Inset text

Bordered text is used to draw attention to important content on the page. This can appear within the flow of body content. This element is detailed in the Government Service Design Manual.

It can take up to 8 weeks to register a lasting power of attorney if there are no mistakes in the application.
{# /example #}

Alert message

Alert messages build upon inset text. These often appear at the top of the page, underneath the main heading. Icons shouldn’t normally be necessary. Alert messages usually span the width of a text column. If you’re layout is full-width, keep the maximum number of characters per line to about 75 (â…” the width of the page at desktop size).

Default alert message

Use this when:

  • a section of text is the most important feature of the page
  • when text needs to be highlighted and standard inset text isn’t enough
  • contact information is of particular significance
Contact Jobcentre Plus to find out how much you owe the Social Fund.
{# /example #}

If you haven’t already booked your Universal Credit interview, please call 035 600 4272 or 0845 600 4272 to arrange a date and time.

Open hours are 8am to 6pm, Monday to Friday.

{# /example #}
Make sure you register within 7 days or you’ll have to register again.
{# /example #}

Warning alert message

These are different to error messages.

Use this when:

  • there’s an issue that a user should know about, but they didn’t cause

You may experience some technical issues when using this application due to ongoing maintenance.

{# /example #}

Positive alert message

These are different to confirmation messages and are quite rare.

Use this when:

  • an action has been completed during a transaction, but the user hasn’t completed their full journey

Application saved

{# /example #}

Legal text

Use bold text with an exclamation icon if there are legal consequences - for example, a fine or prison sentence. This element is detailed in the Government Service Design Manual.

A border is not required around this element and it can be included anywhere within the main content of a page.

{#

Legal text - icon alignment centre

Use this when:

  • messages are short and up to around 75 characters (one line at wide browser viewports)
#}
Warning You can be fined up to £5,000 if you don’t register.
{# /example #}

Use a vertically centred icon when messages are short and up to around 75 characters (1 line at wide browser viewports)

Warning

It is important that you understand that in return for your Universal Credit payment you are agreeing to look for work.

You’ll need to commit to doing everything you reasonably can to find and take paid work. Your work coach will help you agree your commitments.

{# /example #}

Use a top-aligned icon when messages are longer than 75 characters (2 lines or more at wide browser viewports)

Error message

This element is detailed in the Government Service Design Manual and normally appears above the main heading of a page.

Use this when:

  • there’s an error that’s the result of something the user has done
  • a problem has occurred that the user needs to address

There’s a problem

Check your form. You must:

  • Enter your full name
  • Enter a valid email address
{# /example #}

Your partner hasn’t created their own account yet.

They should go to ‘Register with a partner code’ on the homepage and use this code:

Partner code: YF92NEXT

They have 7 days left - the code expires on 26/06/2016

If they don’t use it, you’ll both have to register again.

{# /example #}

Confirmation message

Use this when:

  • a user has completed an action successfully
  • a user has completed a transaction

Your application has been received

Your National Insurance number is your reference

{# /example #}

Invite sent to john.smith@example.com

{# /example #}

You have amended your business details

{# /example #}
