Alternative pattern 1

Use this when:

  • you want the user to consider which account they want the money paid into

Research notes

User research for Get your State Pension found that asking people whether they want to be paid into a bank account or a building society account makes them think about whether they need their pension for everyday spending or for savings.

    $(document).on('click', 'input[type="radio"]', function (e) {
      if ($(e.target).attr('id') === 'bank-account-yes' || $(e.target).attr('id') === 'building-society-yes') {
        $('#bank-details').show();
        if ($(this).attr('id') === 'building-society-yes') {
          $('#building-society-details').show();
        } else {
          $('#building-society-details').hide();
        }
      }
    });

Alternative pattern 2

Use this when:

  • you need to know whether the user has a bank account to receive payment

Research notes

User research for Carer’s Allowance found that some people don’t have a bank account. They needed to be able to complete the transaction without providing any bank details.

Your bank details

Carer’s Allowance can be paid into a UK bank account, either your own or someone else’s, or your own Post Office card account. You’re responsible for repaying any overpayments, even if the money is paid into someone else’s account

Do you have a bank account?
Sort code

You won't be paid Carer's Allowance without a bank account.
You can add bank details after you apply by contacting the Carer's Allowance Unit.
